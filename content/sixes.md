## The Game You Love Just Found a New Gear

**Sixes** is lacrosse distilled to its fastest, most creative form — a 6-on-6 discipline
played on a smaller field with a shot clock and non-stop pace. At R3VA, it's the format
we build around because it puts the ball in every player's stick far more often than the
traditional game.

## What Is Sixes?

World Lacrosse introduced the **6v6 Sixes** discipline in May 2021 as a streamlined,
TV-friendly, globally accessible version of the sport. On the strength of this format, the
**International Olympic Committee granted World Lacrosse Full Recognition** — clearing the
path for lacrosse's return to the **2028 Los Angeles Olympic Games**.

It blends the best of **box and field** lacrosse: the tight spaces and quick decisions of
box, with the open running of field — producing a faster tempo and small-space creativity
that feels closer to **basketball's non-stop flow** than to a traditional 10v10 game.

## Why We Play Sixes

- **Touch the ball ~30% more.** Smaller field, fewer players, faster transitions — every
  player gets more shots, more dodges, more defensive stands, and more saves.
- **Every position develops.** With only five runners, there's nowhere to hide. Kids learn
  to play both ends of the field instead of specializing too early.
- **More reps, less standing around.** A running clock and quick restarts keep the game in
  motion — more lacrosse, less waiting.
- **It's the future of the sport.** The format your kids see in the Olympics is the format
  they'll be playing here.

## Sixes at a Glance

| Element | How It Works |
|---|---|
| **Players** | 6 per side — 5 runners + 1 goalie |
| **Field** | ~70 yds × 40 yds (roughly one-third of a traditional field) |
| **Game length** | Four short running-clock quarters |
| **Shot clock** | 30 seconds to get a shot off |
| **Scoring** | One point per goal |
| **Faceoffs** | Draw to start each quarter; possession awarded after goals |
| **Positions** | No offsides — all field players roam end to end |
| **Sticks** | All short sticks; fast self-start restarts keep play moving |

> Rules above reflect the general World Lacrosse Sixes framework. Exact game length,
> divisions, and any youth modifications vary by tournament — check the specific event page
> for details.
